Come to Charleston

A favorite for locals and visitors alike is White Point Garden, sometimes called "Battery Park."
Where you will find amazing large oak trees, several Civil War relics and memorials. Kids love to
climb on the cannons which makes for a great photo. From there you can take a lovely stroll down
the Charleston harbor with a view of the water on one side and antebellum mansions on the other.

Charleston is also known for their Historic Horse and Carriage Tours, learn more about Charleston's rich history
on a guided tour of the "Holy City". Large beautiful draft horses will take you down cobblestone streets
on a scenic route of the city, with views of mansions, enchanting gardens and churches.

Another iconic stop in Charleston is the Pineapple Fountain located in the heart of Waterfront Park.
Where you'll find panoramic views of the Charleston Harbor, two large fountains fantastic for photo opportunities and fun
for all ages to splash in and cool off. Walking down to the pier that extends into the Cooper River take a break in the
covered family size swings. At the far end of the pier gaze at the Arthur Ravenel Junior Bridge, the USS Yorktown, Castle Pinckney
and Fort Sumter. Here you can take a Charleston water taxi to see and photograph some friendly dolphins on the way to Patriot Point
and back. Relax and enjoy the views of historic sites from the water and see some of Charleston's most visited attractions.
